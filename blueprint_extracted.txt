Story  3.1 The World  Lumina is a mysterious glowing world hidden beyond the edges of maps. No one knows how it was created. No one knows who built the ancient structures inside it. What everyone knows is this: somewhere deep inside Lumina, there is one treasure. It glows brighter than anything in the world. It is worth more than anything in the world. And only one team can take it home.  3.2 The Teams  Two teams are sent into Lumina at the same time. They do not know exactly where the treasure is. They do not know the map   —   because every time someone enters Lumina, the world rearranges itself. The paths are different. The landmarks are different. The treasure is hidden somewhere new. Both teams start from opposite sides of the world and begin searching immediately.  3.3 The Treasure  The treasure is one single glowing object sitting somewhere in the world. The moment any player touches it and picks it up, a golden light shoots into the sky and a timer begins. Everyone in both teams sees the light. Everyone knows the treasure has been found. Now the real game begins.  3.4 The Fight  The team that picked up the treasure must carry it to the exit door and walk through before the timer hits zero. But the other team will not let that happen. They chase. They fight. They try to knock the treasure out of the carrier's hands and grab it themselves. If they succeed, now they are the ones running for the exit   —   and the original team is chasing them.  3.5 The Outcome  Three things can happen. First: the team carrying the treasure reaches the exit and walks through. They win. The treasure is theirs. The other team watches them leave. Second: the other team steals the treasure and exits before the timer runs out. They win instead. The first team lost what they found. Third: the timer runs out and nobody made it to the exit. The treasure stays in Lumina. Nobody wins. It is a draw. Lumina keeps its secret one more time.  3.6 The Tone

Map & World Design  6.1 Map Philosophy  Every match in Lumina is played on a unique procedurally generated map. No two games share the same layout. Players cannot memorize the map to gain an advantage. Every match requires fresh exploration. The map generator guarantees that every generated map is fair: both teams have equal path distance to the treasure spawn area, the exit door is always reachable, and no player can be permanently trapped.  6.2 Map Structure  Element   Specification  Map Shape   Rectangular grid of chunks. Size: 24×24 chunks at 16m each = 384m×384m total.  Chunk Size   16×16 meters. Each chunk is one tile in the grid.  Biomes   3 biomes per map: Forest (40%), Ruins (35%), Crystal Caves (25%). Distributed in contiguous regions.  Team A Spawn   Northwest quadrant. At least 120m from treasure spawn zone.  Team B Spawn   Southeast quadrant. At least 120m from treasure spawn zone.  Treasure Spawn Zone   Central 8×8 chunk area of the map. Exact tile selected randomly within zone.  Exit Door   Placed after treasure spawn is determined. Equidistant from both team spawns (within 10% margin).  Path Guarantee   BFS run from every spawn to treasure and from treasure to exit. Map rejected and regenerated if any path fails.  Obstacles   20 – 35% of chunks are blocked (walls, cliffs, water). Never blocks critical paths.  Landmarks   3 – 5 unique visual landmarks placed per map for player orientation.  6.3 Biome Details  Biome   Visual   Gameplay Effect  Whispering Forest   Dense trees, glowing mushrooms, rivers, soft amber light  Open sightlines blocked by trees. Good for ambushes.  Ancient Ruins   Crumbled stone structures, broken columns, overgrown paths  Vertical geometry for hiding. Multiple route options.  Crystal Caves   Glowing crystal formations, narrow passages, low ceilings  Tight corridors. Hard to dodge attacks. High tension.

Confidential   —   Internal Production Document  6.4 Procedural Generation Algorithm  Step 1   —   Initialize Grid  •   Create 24×24 grid of empty chunks.  •   Mark Team A spawn (NW quadrant 3×3 chunks) and Team B spawn (SE quadrant 3×3 chunks).  •   Mark Treasure Zone (center 8×8 chunks).  Step 2   —   Place Biomes  •   Assign biome regions using cellular automata seeded with a random value.  •   Ensure each biome covers its minimum percentage of the map.  •   Smooth biome borders with 3 passes of cellular automata.  Step 3   —   Place Obstacles  •   Randomly block 20 – 35% of non-spawn, non-treasure-zone chunks.  •   Run BFS from Team A spawn to Treasure Zone   —   clear any blocking chunks on the critical path.  •   Run BFS from Team B spawn to Treasure Zone   —   clear any blocking chunks on the critical path.  Step 4   —   Place Treasure  •   Select a random chunk within the Treasure Zone that is not adjacent to either team's spawn.  •   Place treasure at the center of that chunk.  Step 5   —   Place Exit Door  •   Find the chunk that is approximately equidistant from both team spawns and at least 80m from treasure.  •   Place exit door at center of that chunk.  •   Run BFS from treasure to exit   —   clear blocking chunks on this path.  Step 6   —   Validate  •   BFS: Team A spawn → Treasure. Must find path. If not: restart generation.  •   BFS: Team B spawn → Treasure. Must find path. If not: restart generation.  •   BFS: Treasure → Exit. Must find path. If not: restart generation.  •   If 3 restarts fail: use fallback map from hardcoded library of 10 pre-validated maps.  Step 7   —   Decorate  •   Place props, trees, rocks, and decorations in non-blocked chunks based on biome rules.  •   Place 3 – 5 landmarks.  •   Generate lightmap data.  6.5 Map Seed  Every map is generated from a random integer seed. The seed is generated by the server when the match starts. The seed is sent to all clients so they generate the exact same map locally. The server keeps its own authoritative copy for collision and navigation calculations.  Map seed is included in the post-match summary so players can share memorable maps. Future roadmap: replay system  using seed.
